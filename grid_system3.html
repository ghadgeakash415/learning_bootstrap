The foundation of the Army's structure, a squad comprises 8 to 12 soldiers. Typically, a sergeant or staff sergeant leads the unit.Platoons: This is a collection of squads, usually made up of 26 to 55 soldiers. A platoon is headed by a lieutenant with a senior non-commissioned officer as his second-in-command.The primary mission of the Indian Army is to ensure national security and unity, defend the nation from external aggression and internal threats, and maintain peace and security within its borders. Companies: Companies consist of multiple platoons, bringing the soldier count to between 80 and 250. The leadership generally falls to a captain or major.
